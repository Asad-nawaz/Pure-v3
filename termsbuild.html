Keywords are NOT case sensitive

Type your keyword or phrase, and click “enter” or “return”

to save (input more than one word to create a “phrase” [for example “accounts receivable”])

Drag & Drop Keywords to the appropriate field: “Desired”, “Required” or “Exclude.”

Use the “Desired” field for most of your Keywords. Two reasons to use the “Desired” field:

  1. “Required” eliminates qualified resumes simply because they do not contain the term spelled exactly as you have it.
  2. To return the greater number of resumes, many job boards will convert your “Required” terms into “Desired”. This is very common.

Suggestion: Avoid “Required” for your first search. The more terms you “Require”, the fewer qualified resumes you will receive. After you run a search, you can always edit a search and rerun with “Required” terms, if you need.

“Desired” supports multiple conditions

(click the icon to open a new condition). This is for advanced Boolean searches. Each nested “Desired” condition is grouped by the “AND” Boolean Operator. Examples of nested conditions:

(RN or registered nurse) AND (ER or emergency room)

In this example, at least 1 term from the first condition MUST appear in all documents, AND 1 term from the second condition MUST also appear; if both conditions are NOT met, the resume will not display.

(manager OR lead OR supervisor) AND (insurance OR finance OR investments) AND (tax OR audit OR "accounts payable")

Synonyms:

Two candidates with the same experience will write their job title and skills differently. Use general terms all qualified candidates will use; cast a net that will capture all possible variations. Examples:

  1. Accounts Receivable – input these terms into “Desired” for best results: “accounts receivable”, “accounts receivables”, “account receivable”, “account receivables”
    NOTE: We input 4 variations (“plural / singular”, “plural / plural”, “singular / singular”, “singular / plural”)
  2. Database Administrator – input these terms into “Desired” for best results: “dba”, “database administrator”, “data base administrator”
    NOTE: the spelling of database & data base – to capture all possible spellings

Skills:

List each skill separately, in the “Desired” field. Do NOT attach skills to a job title. For example: “QuickBooks Accountant” will exclude resumes without this exact phrase; for better results input both terms separately in the “Desired” field (e.g. “QuickBooks” or “Accountant”).

Wild Card (*) Searches:

Every job board search interprets the * differently. For best results across the entire network of databases, we highly recommend avoiding the *; instead, include each variation of your term in the “Desired” field.
